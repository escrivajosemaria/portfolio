Core Focus Areas

Primary Health Care Systems

Primary health care (PHC) is widely recognized as the most efficient, equitable, and cost-effective foundation for strong health systems globally. It is the approach through which universal health coverage (UHC) and the health-related Sustainable Development Goals become achievable, because PHC meets people‚Äôs needs as close as possible to where they live, from health promotion to prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation. Scaling up comprehensive PHC across low- and middle-income countries could avert an estimated 60‚ÄØmillion deaths and increase average life expectancy by nearly four years by 2030. Integrated PHC also strengthens resilience to health crises and reduces inequities by addressing broader determinants of health and engaging communities as active partners in health decisions.

Vaccination & Disease Prevention

Vaccination remains one of the greatest public health successes of modern times, saving an estimated 3.5‚ÄØmillion to 5‚ÄØmillion lives each year by preventing diseases such as diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis, influenza, and measles. Immunization services support primary health care by keeping communities healthy and connecting people to essential health providers, while also contributing to broader disease prevention and health security. However, coverage gaps persist: millions of children worldwide still miss critical vaccines, leaving populations vulnerable to outbreaks that can rapidly exploit weaknesses in health systems. Sustaining and improving vaccination coverage involves tailoring immunization strategies to local contexts, strengthening supply chains, and integrating preventive care into everyday health services. More details can be found on the WHO Vaccines & Immunization page.

Community Health Programs

Community health initiatives are not just add-ons to formal health systems; they are central to achieving population-wide health impact. Effective community health programs empower local populations to identify priorities, shape interventions, and sustain momentum for healthier outcomes. Evidence shows that when community health worker (CHW) programs are well integrated into national systems, they can address gaps in access and equity, especially in resource-constrained settings. CHWs play a crucial role in delivering promotive, preventive, and basic curative services, bridging gaps between formal facilities and the people they serve. Their strategic engagement contributes to early detection, risk communication, and sustained uptake of essential health interventions. Read more at BMC Health Policy & Systems.

Health Data & Reporting

Data are the backbone of effective health decision-making. Without reliable information on births, deaths, disease trends, and service delivery, policies and programs risk being misaligned with real needs. Initiatives like the CDC Data for Health Initiative have helped partner countries improve civil registration and vital statistics systems, capturing tens of millions of previously unrecorded birth and death records. Strong health information systems‚Äîincluding routine health facility data, community reporting, and interoperable platforms‚Äîenable timely analysis, resource prioritization, and adaptive responses to emerging challenges. When frontline data are routinely incorporated into decision cycles, health systems become more responsive, accountable, and capable of driving equitable outcomes.
