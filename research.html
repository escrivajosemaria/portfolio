Research & Publications

Mar 2025 – Jul 2025
Anchoring the SDGs in Moral Leadership: East Africa’s Development Through Mandela’s Lens

This research examines how the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are operationalized across East Africa, focusing on the gap between global development commitments and on-the-ground realities. Using a moral leadership framework inspired by Nelson Mandela, the study assesses progress and systemic constraints across poverty reduction, healthcare access, education, climate resilience, and governance. The project offers a critical evaluation of implementation mechanisms, monitoring practices, and institutional accountability, generating insights aimed at strengthening policy coherence and advancing the region’s progress toward the 2030 Agenda.

Catholic University of Eastern Africa
Unraveling Factors Predisposing Young Adults Aged 18–25 to Mental Health Problems

This publication investigates the structural, social, and psychological factors contributing to mental health challenges among university-aged young adults. Drawing on empirical data, the study explores how academic pressure, social expectations, economic uncertainty, and limited mental health support systems intersect to elevate risks of stress, anxiety, and depression. Beyond diagnosis, the research calls for systemic reform in how institutions address youth mental well-being, positioning mental health as a public health and development priority rather than an individual failing.

Co-Author | Unpublished Research
Determinants of Maternal Healthcare Services Utilization among Women of Reproductive Age in Gataka, Nkaimurunya Sub-County, Kajiado County, Kenya

This study examines the socio-economic, cultural, and health system factors influencing maternal healthcare utilization among women of reproductive age in a peri-urban Kenyan setting. The research explores barriers to antenatal care attendance, skilled birth delivery, and postnatal services, with particular attention to access, education, household decision-making, and health facility responsiveness. By grounding maternal health outcomes within local context, the study contributes evidence to inform targeted interventions aimed at improving maternal service uptake and reducing preventable maternal morbidity and mortality.

Research Approach

Research is approached as a tool for strengthening health systems, informing programs, and improving population outcomes. The objective is not research for publication alone, but the generation of credible evidence that can be applied to policy decisions, program design, and community-level action. Each stage of the research process is intentionally structured to ensure rigor, relevance, and real-world utility.

Overall Research Goals

The overarching goal across all research activities is to produce evidence that is methodologically sound, contextually grounded, and directly applicable to health system improvement. Research is designed to identify gaps in service delivery, understand behavioral and structural drivers of health outcomes, and support evidence-informed decision-making at community, facility, and system levels.

Emphasis is placed on practical relevance, ethical rigor, and clear translation of findings into action. This reflects a belief widely supported in applied public health research that evidence achieves impact only when it informs implementation, policy, and learning.

Open to research collaboration, co-authorship, and applied studies. Also available to support teams seeking a research assistant for design, analysis, and writing.
